My path into DevOps was not a straight line — it started in telecom networking, designing FTTH and 5G backbone systems at Cyient. That's where I learned to think in systems: throughput, failure domains, and what happens at 2 a.m. when something breaks.

At ITOrizon I moved into DevOps proper — Terraform, Ansible, containerized services, CI/CD pipelines, and a 25% downtime reduction I'm still proud of. At NJ Transit I built cloud-hosted backend services and dashboards giving visibility into 800+ production servers.

Alongside my Master's, I published research on RAG pipelines and Transformer-based LLMs at ASEE-NE — because I believe the future of infrastructure is intelligent, not just automated.
